1. Abstract

Nonsuicidal self-injury (NSSI) refers to the intentional self-inflicted destruction of body tissue without suicidal intention and for purposes not socially sanctioned. Self-harm means nonsuicidal, self-injurious behaviors.

2. Introduction

Nonsuicidal Self-Injury (NSSI) behavior is a growing clinical and public health problem. NSSI is defined as the direct and deliberate destruction of one's own bodily tissue in the absence of lethal intent and for reasons not socially sanctioned (Favazza, 1996; Nock, 2010). Common forms of NSSI include behaviors such as cutting, burning, scratching, and self-hitting (Briere and Gil, 1998; Laye-Gindhu and Schonert-Reichl, 2005; Whitlock et al., 2006; Klonsky and Muehlenkamp, 2007) and most self-injurers report using multiple method (Favazza and Conterio, 1988; Favazza, 1992). Evidences focused on the psychological intentions underlying NSSI demonstrated that the behavior serves a variety of function, both interpersonal and intrapersonal, that are not mutually exclusive.

This project is supposed to enable a simple way for humans to detect and to help people when their mental health is suffering. How is this working? You write in the predict section the twitter username of the person you want to verify. The algorithm is checking their posts and is searching for patterns, similar with the data it was trained into. After checking a verdict is set. I proposed to myself to make the algorithm as close to 100% corectness as it is possible. Of course, there might be mistakes since humans use sarcasm or masking and the machine that learns some patterns might be fooled.

Purpose of the algorithm:

  • falls into the category of health
  • it is made for every person that wants to know the chance of self-harm of another
  • brings to the table a simple way of prediction of self-harm

4 Structure (Website pages)

4.1 Home page

This page connects the others trough a easy to navigate menu and gives a short description of the utility of the website.

4.2 Contact us page

This page has a form to complete in order the contact the team behind the algorithm. The form is supposed to send an email to the responsible pf the website.

4.3 Predict page

In this page the user writes the twitter username of the person that needs to be verified with the algorithm. The algortihm is checking the posts of the user in order to decide if he or she is suspected of self harm behaviours.

4.4 Inspiration & Help page

This page mentions the articles used to understand better the topic in order to create the algorithm. It also presents the sites to help the person with the self-harm behaviour.

5. Concepts/Ideas

5.1 Simplicity

This idea is represented an easy to navigate menu and big intuitive text to guide the user trough the website pages.

5.2 Empathy

This idea is represented by the page "Inspiration & Help " that emotionally connects with the user trough sincere explations regarding why I chose to implement this algorithm.

5.3 Problem-Solver

This idea is represented by the page "Inspiration & Help " that has recommendations of other websites that can help the person with that behaviour.
